Worship & Giving

In the New Testament, Paul reminds Corinthian Christians of the motivating principle for their generosity.  He writes: “For you know the generous act of our Lord Jesus Christ,
that though he was rich, yet for your sakes he became poor, so that by his poverty you might become rich.” (2 Corinthians 8.9)

Jesus gave up all of his eternal wealth that we might share in his riches.  Therefore, it is out of an experience and awareness of this endless gift that we give to others.  In doing so we learn generosity and joyfulness.  Understanding the riches we possess in Christ frees us from unhealthy concern for our possessions and motivates us to invest our resources in God’s eternal kingdom.

At All Souls it is our practice to offer a sacrificial portion of all of our resources—time, skills, possessions—as an act of faith and gratitude in response to God’s grace. We give the church the opportunity to give during our worship services, because it is a tangible act of worship, an expression of deep gratitude to God for his generosity to us in Christ.
